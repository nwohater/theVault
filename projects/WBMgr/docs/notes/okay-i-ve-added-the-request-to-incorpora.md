Product Requirements Document — describe what you want to build, the problem it solves, and success criteria.

**1. Overview**

Wiffle Ball Manager (WBM) is a fantasy wiffle ball game management application. Players will draft teams, manage their rosters, track stats, and compete against other players.

**2. Goals**

*   Provide a fun and engaging fantasy wiffle ball experience.
*   Offer a robust and customizable game management system.
*   Foster a community around the game.

**3. Features**

*   **Team Creation & Drafting:** Users can create teams and draft wiffle ball players from a pool.
*   **Roster Management:** Users can manage their team rosters, including making trades and dropping players.
*   **Game Simulation:** An automated game simulation engine will determine the outcome of each wiffle ball matchup.
*   **Stats Tracking:** Detailed stats will be tracked for each player and team.
*   **League Management:**  Features for creating and managing leagues (public and private).
*   **Leaderboards:**  Display team and player rankings based on performance.
*   **Player Modal:** A modal window to view detailed player information.
*   **Settings & Local AI Chat:** Allows users to customize their experience and interact with an AI assistant.

**4. Developmental Teams**

*   **Concept:** To encourage ongoing engagement and provide a testing ground for new features, each WBM league will include a set of "Developmental Teams." These teams will function similarly to minor league baseball teams.
*   **Gameplay:** Developmental teams will play against each other in simulated games.  This provides a low-pressure environment for testing new mechanics, stats, or UI elements.
*   **Purpose:**  Data gathered from these developmental games will be used to inform future updates and improvements to the main WBM game.  It will also be a space to experiment with new features before broader release.

**5. Success Criteria**

*   High user engagement and retention.
*   Positive user feedback regarding gameplay and features.
*   Successful identification and testing of new features through the developmental team system.
